# Business Insights

Format: **Observation → Reason → Business Impact → Recommendation → Priority**
All figures are real outputs from this project's data (7,043 customers).

---

### 1. Month-to-month contracts churn 15x more than two-year contracts
- **Observation:** Month-to-month churn is 42.71% vs 2.83% for two-year contracts.
- **Reason:** No commitment/switching cost — customers can leave anytime with zero penalty.
- **Impact:** Month-to-month customers make up the majority of the 1,869 total churned accounts.
- **Recommendation:** Offer a modest discount (e.g. 5-10%) to convert month-to-month customers to 1-year contracts at renewal touchpoints.
- **Priority: Critical**

### 2. Nearly half of all first-year customers churn
- **Observation:** 47.44% churn rate in the 0-1 year tenure bucket, dropping to 9.51% at 4+ years.
- **Reason:** Onboarding/early-experience gap — customers haven't yet formed switching habits or seen long-term value.
- **Impact:** The first 12 months is the single highest-leverage retention window.
- **Recommendation:** Build a structured 90-day onboarding check-in program (welcome call, usage tips, proactive support outreach).
- **Priority: Critical**

### 3. Electronic check payers churn 3x more than credit card autopay users
- **Observation:** 45.29% churn for electronic check vs 15.24% for credit card (automatic).
- **Reason:** Manual payment methods correlate with lower commitment/engagement; automatic payments reduce "friction moments" where a customer reconsiders.
- **Impact:** Electronic check is likely the most common payment method among churned users.
- **Recommendation:** Incentivize migration to autopay (small bill credit for switching) — proven retention lever in subscription businesses.
- **Priority: High**

### 4. Fiber optic customers churn more than double DSL customers
- **Observation:** 41.89% churn for Fiber optic vs 18.96% for DSL, despite Fiber being the premium/higher-priced tier.
- **Reason:** Likely price-value perception gap or service reliability complaints — needs a targeted customer satisfaction survey to confirm root cause.
- **Impact:** This is the highest-revenue service tier, so churn here has outsized dollar impact.
- **Recommendation:** Commission a Fiber-specific NPS/CSAT survey before assuming price is the sole driver; don't discount blindly without diagnosis.
- **Priority: High**

### 5. No tech support = 41.64% churn vs 15.17% with tech support
- **Observation:** Nearly identical pattern for Online Security (41.77% vs 14.61%) and Online Backup.
- **Reason:** These add-ons increase switching cost (more integrated into daily use) and directly address the two biggest friction points (security worry, tech frustration).
- **Impact:** Under-adopted add-ons represent a low-cost, high-leverage retention lever.
- **Recommendation:** Bundle a free 3-month Tech Support trial into all new month-to-month sign-ups.
- **Priority: High**

### 6. More subscribed services generally correlates with lower churn
- **Observation:** Churn drops from 42.86% (0 services) to 5.29% (8 services), though not perfectly monotonic.
- **Reason:** Bundling increases both switching cost and perceived value-for-money.
- **Impact:** Confirms a cross-sell strategy is also a retention strategy, not just a revenue play.
- **Recommendation:** Sales team should be measured on "services per customer," not just new sign-ups.
- **Priority: Medium**

### 7. Senior citizens churn nearly twice as often as non-seniors
- **Observation:** 41.68% vs 23.61% churn.
- **Reason:** Possibly higher price sensitivity, more complex billing/tech, or targeted competitor promotions for this demographic.
- **Impact:** A meaningful, identifiable segment for a tailored retention approach.
- **Recommendation:** Simplify billing communication and offer a dedicated senior-support line.
- **Priority: Medium**

### 8. Customers without partners/dependents churn most
- **Observation:** "No partner, no dependents" churns at 34.24% vs 14.24% for "partner + dependents."
- **Reason:** Single-person households may be more price-sensitive and have fewer switching-cost anchors (e.g., no shared family plan).
- **Impact:** A demographic worth testing family/multi-line bundle offers against.
- **Recommendation:** Test a "bring a friend/family member" bundle discount targeted at single-account holders.
- **Priority: Medium**

### 9. $139,085/month (30.49% of total MRR) is sitting with already-churned customers
- **Observation:** Revenue-weighted churn (30.49%) is meaningfully higher than customer-count churn (26.54%).
- **Reason:** Churned customers skew toward higher MonthlyCharges ($74.42 avg) than retained customers ($61.27 avg) — the company is disproportionately losing its higher-paying customers.
- **Impact:** This is not just a customer-count problem, it's a revenue-quality problem.
- **Recommendation:** Prioritize retention spend on high-value accounts, not just high-churn-probability accounts — the two aren't the same population.
- **Priority: Critical**

### 10. Paperless billing customers churn 2x more than paper-bill customers
- **Observation:** 33.57% vs 16.33% churn.
- **Reason:** Counter-intuitive — worth investigating whether this reflects a demographic (younger, more price-comparison-savvy) rather than the billing format itself causing churn.
- **Impact:** A useful segmentation variable, but likely a proxy for something else (age, tech-savviness, contract type overlap).
- **Recommendation:** Don't act on this variable directly — cross-tabulate with contract type and age before drawing causal conclusions.
- **Priority: Low (needs further analysis, flagged for rigor)**
